Proverb of the Day

قلم از شمشیر برنده‌تر است

The pen is mightier than the sword

This proverb suggests that thinking and writing have more influence than violence and force. It emphasizes the power of communication and ideas to change the world in ways that physical force cannot.

هر که نان از عمل خود خورد، منت حاتم نبرد

Whoever earns their bread through their own work, doesn’t owe favors to the generous.

This proverb teaches the value of independence and hard work. It reminds us that relying on our own efforts brings dignity and freedom from dependence.

کسی که خوابیده را می‌شود بیدار کرد، نه کسی که خود را به خواب زده

You can wake up someone who is asleep, but not someone pretending to sleep.

This highlights the difficulty of helping someone who chooses ignorance. You can only guide those willing to listen, not those deliberately avoiding the truth.

نیش مار از دشمن نیست، از دوست ناباب است

The sting of a snake is not from an enemy, but from a false friend.

Betrayal by a trusted person hurts more than any enemy's action. It warns us to be careful about whom we trust and value true friendship.

درختی که ریشه‌اش عمیق است، از طوفان نمی‌ترسد

A tree with deep roots fears no storm.

A strong person with deep inner strength can handle any challenge without falling.
